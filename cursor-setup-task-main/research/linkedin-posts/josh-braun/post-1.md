## Content
Cold calling.

The first 90 seconds.

Step 1: Lean back and detach.

When your intent is to book a meeting, you say things that feel pushy.

That’s because your intent affects your behavior.

Whenever prospects feel the push, they enter the Zone of Resistance.

The more you explain, the less people trust you.

Your solution has no value without a problem, so it’s madness to explain right out of the gate.

Your intent?

“Is there a problem?”

That shift changes your energy.

When you detach, you sound relaxed and chill, so people are more likely to lower their guard.

Step 2: Open the conversation.

“Hey John, this is Mandy Jones.

We’ve never spoken, and I know you weren’t expecting my call, but I was hoping you could help me out for a moment.”

Step 3: Make sure you’re talking to the right person.

“Are you still responsible for calculating payouts for reps?”

Step 4: Poke the bear.

Ask an A, B, or C question that illuminates a potential problem.

Make people feel smart.

“Not sure if you’re seeing this, but when commissions run through Excel, reps often lack visibility into how payouts are calculated, which can lead to disputes.

How are your reps seeing where their number came from today?

Are you sending PDFs, walking them through spreadsheets, or can reps log in anytime and see how their payout number was derived?”

A = Current way
B = Current way
C = What’s possible

It’s not your job to fill people’s heads with information.

Your job is to draw it out.

Buyers have the answers.

Sellers have the questions.
