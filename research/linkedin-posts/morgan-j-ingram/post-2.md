## Content
I caught myself doing something last week that scared me. Was struggling with a personal problem.. and instead of solving it myself.. my first instinct was to open Claude and ask it what to say.

Which is wild.. because I was like wait I am starting to outsource my thinking.

That's when I realized how deep this goes.

So I took a staycation this past weekend.. And during that staycation I made one rule.. no running to AI for answers.

Here's my rules with AI moving forward. 

𝟭. 𝗣𝗿𝗮𝘆 𝗳𝗶𝗿𝘀𝘁.
Some of you may not vibe with this and that's totally fine. I'm just telling you what I am doing now.. When I have a concern or need clarity.. I am going to pray about it FIRST... And every single time I have done this in the past.. I felt better so time to double down. 

𝟮. 𝗪𝗿𝗶𝘁𝗲 𝗼𝘂𝘁 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗽𝗿𝗼𝗯𝗹𝗲𝗺 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝘁𝗿𝘆 𝘁𝗼 𝘀𝗼𝗹𝘃𝗲 𝗶𝘁 𝗺𝘆𝘀𝗲𝗹𝗳.

Before opening any AI tool I am going to grab a notebook and write out what the actual problem is. Funny enough, when I did this I started feeling better... my brain was locked in. I think in this AI world we forget to slow down. 

𝟯. 𝗖𝗮𝗹𝗹 𝘀𝗼𝗺𝗲𝗼𝗻𝗲 𝘄𝗵𝗼 𝗵𝗮𝘀 𝗿𝗲𝗮𝗹 𝗰𝗼𝗻𝘁𝗲𝘅𝘁.

Call someone who has experienced the thing that you are going through. Most people give advice based on their own environment.. if you are going to get an opinion get the right one...

Then sure.. after all these three steps if I want to brainstorm with AI.. have it! However, that is step 4 not step 1. 

I'm not anti-AI. I use it every single day for my business.

I am just saying.. if I am going there for every problem.. question.. decision.. it becomes a crutch.

And this applies to your selling too...
 
If you're an SDR/AE and your first move on every prospect is to ask AI what to say.. you're outsourcing the one thing that makes you valuable. Your thinking. Your observation. Your ability to connect the dots.
 
The B2B sales teams who win right now aren't the ones with the best prompts... They're the ones who can look at a prospect's LinkedIn.. website... notice to connect the dots with.. and start a conversation that sounds like a human wrote it. Because a human did.

AI is a mirror. 

But you have to look in your own mirror first.

P.S. Has anyone else caught themselves doing this? I can't be the only one...

Personalization in cold outreach should go beyond surface-level details and focus on relevance. Messaging that connects directly to a prospect’s current priorities or challenges is more likely to generate engagement than generic templates.
